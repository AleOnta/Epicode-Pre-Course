Valmalenco

Lombardia, Italy

Author: Alessandro Ontani

Valmalenco (Malenco Valley) is an area of the Italian Central Alps, next to the the Swiss border and is very rich in both aesthetic specimens and rare mineral species.

It is famous for andradite (variety demantoid), quartz with magnesite and tiragalloite specimens. The Valley hosts several historical mineral locations, and is also a beautiful place with typical Alpine landscapes. The reason for such a mineralogical richness mainly sources from its geological variability. Different species occur in various different geological units that include metamorphic and magmatic rocks. Many interesting outcrops are present at high elevation(2000-4000 m above sea level) but minerals can be also found in erratics spread over the valley (like Val Sissone and Vallone di Scerscen) and in the glacial moraines left by the retreating glaciers. Many "classic" localities are quite widely exploited, but the extension of the high altitude areas, only accessible in Summer allowed local collectors to find good specimens, even in relatively recent times.
